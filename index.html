Justin van Dijk. Mapping Dutchman.

Welcome. I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Geospatial Analytics and Computing Group, at the Department of Geography, University College London. Here, I am part of the Urban Dynamics Lab, a five year EPSRC-funded research project bringing together expertise from across UCL (Space Syntax, Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis, Geography and Computer Science), to explore and address questions at the intersection of city and regional economic development and urban modelling, data analytics and computing. Below you will find some details on the latest research projects and plan I have been involved in.

I am mainly interested in urban analytics, geo-demographics, urban transport, spatial inequalities, and big data applications in scarce data environments. Extensive experience in the domain of Geographic Information Systems, urban studies, and transport research. Possesses a wide skill set in the field of advanced spatial analysis, including experience with Python programming, R scripting, and PostgreSQL/PostGIS.

Urban Analytics. Residential Mobility in the United Kingdom.

Re-purposing consumer and administrative data in order to develop robust annual estimates of residential mobility between all UK neighbourhoods

GBNames. Interactive display of names distributions.

Exploring the generational and inter-generational residential movements of family groups across Great Britain.

Spatial Inequalities. Demographic change in Great Britain.

Summarising intergenerational change in local populations across Great Britain over a period of 120 years.

Urban Transport and Mobility. Informal transport in South Africa.

Representing "underrepresented" mobilities by harnessing consumer data in urban environments with a poor data infrastructure to generate scale-free and highly granular insights into social and spatial inequalities.
